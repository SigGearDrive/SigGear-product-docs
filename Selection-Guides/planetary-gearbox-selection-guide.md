# Planetary Gearbox Selection Guide

Selecting the right planetary gearbox is important for compact motion systems that require speed reduction, torque increase, stable transmission, and reliable motor integration.

SigGear provides miniature and compact planetary gear reducers from 6mm to 42mm for micro robotics, robotic grippers, medical devices, laboratory automation, optical equipment, servo motors, stepper motors, brushless motors, DC motors, compact actuators, and industrial automation systems.

This guide helps engineers understand how to choose a suitable planetary gearbox based on size, torque, speed, gear ratio, backlash, motor type, shaft design, mounting interface, noise, duty cycle, and customization requirements.

## What Is a Planetary Gearbox?

A planetary gearbox is a compact gear reducer that uses a sun gear, planet gears, and ring gear to reduce motor speed and increase output torque.

Planetary gearboxes are widely used because they offer:

* Compact structure
* Stable torque transmission
* Flexible gear ratio options
* Good torque density
* Motor integration flexibility
* Wide size range
* Suitable structure for automation and robotics
* Custom shaft and mounting options

For compact products, a planetary gearbox helps engineers reduce speed, increase torque, and fit the drive system into limited installation space.

A suitable planetary gearbox helps improve:

## Step-by-Step Planetary Gearbox Selection Process

### Step 1: Define the Application

Start with the actual application.

Useful questions:

* Is it used with a servo motor?
* Is it used with a DC motor?
* Is it used with a brushless motor?
* Is it used with a stepper motor?
* Is it for a robotic gripper?
* Is it for a medical device?
* Is it for a micro robot?
* Is it for automation equipment?
* Is it for prototype testing or mass production?

Different applications may require different gearbox sizes, ratios, backlash levels, shaft designs, and noise requirements.

## Step 2: Confirm Installation Space

Planetary gearbox selection often starts with mechanical space.

Confirm:

* Maximum outer diameter
* Maximum gearbox length
* Total motor gearbox assembly length
* Output shaft length
* Output shaft diameter
* Mounting hole position
* Flange size
* Cable or wire outlet space
* Encoder or brake space
* Allowed weight

SigGear supports 6-42mm miniature and compact planetary gear reducers for different installation spaces.

## Step 3: Confirm Required Output Torque

Output torque determines whether the gearbox can drive the mechanism reliably.

Engineers should confirm:

* Continuous output torque
* Peak output torque
* Starting torque
* Holding torque
* Safety factor
* Friction load
* Load changes during operation
* Shock load or impact load
* Duty cycle

For robotic grippers, output torque affects gripping force.
For medical pumps and valves, output torque affects reliable actuation.
For servo motor systems, output torque affects load handling and motion stability.

## Step 4: Confirm Required Output Speed

The gearbox reduces motor speed to the required output speed.

Confirm:

* Required output speed
* Motor rated speed
* Motor maximum speed
* Motion cycle time
* Start-stop frequency
* Acceleration and deceleration requirements
* Low-speed smoothness requirement

A higher gear ratio usually provides higher output torque but lower output speed.

## Step 5: Choose the Gear Ratio

Gear ratio selection affects torque, speed, efficiency, noise, and control response.

| Gear Ratio Choice | Typical Result                                                |
| ----------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Lower ratio       | Higher output speed, lower torque multiplication              |
| Medium ratio      | Balanced speed, torque, and efficiency                        |
| Higher ratio      | Higher output torque and lower output speed                   |
| Very high ratio   | More torque but possibly lower efficiency and slower response |

The best gear ratio depends on motor speed, required output speed, required torque, efficiency, backlash, and duty cycle.

## Step 6: Check Backlash Requirement

Backlash is important for positioning and repeatability.

| Application                    | Backlash Sensitivity |
| ------------------------------ | -------------------- |
| Servo motor positioning system | High                 |
| Robotic gripper                | Medium to high       |
| Medical device mechanism       | Medium to high       |
| Optical adjustment system      | High                 |
| Laboratory automation          | Medium to high       |
| Smart hardware                 | Medium               |
| Pump or valve                  | Low to medium        |
| Simple automation mechanism    | Low to medium        |

Lower backlash is usually better for precision motion, but the final requirement depends on the control method, mechanical structure, cost target, and product application.

## Step 7: Match the Motor Type

Planetary gearboxes can be matched with different motor types.

| Motor Type      | Typical Use                                                |
| --------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------- |
| DC motor        | Smart hardware, pumps, valves, small mechanisms            |
| Brushless motor | Medical devices, lab automation, long-life compact systems |
| Stepper motor   | Positioning systems, instruments, automation modules       |
| Servo motor     | Robotics, automation, precision motion systems             |
| Custom motor    | Application-specific compact drive solution                |

Motor matching should consider input shaft size, motor speed, flange size, mounting holes, motor power, control method, encoder, brake, and total assembly size.

## Step 8: Check Shaft and Mounting Design

The output shaft and mounting interface directly affect mechanical integration.

Common output shaft options include:

* Round shaft
* D-shaft
* Flat shaft
* Threaded shaft
* Gear shaft
* Spline shaft
* Custom shaft diameter
* Custom shaft length
* Custom output gear

Common mounting options include:

* Screw hole mounting
* Flange mounting
* Bracket mounting
* Housing integration
* Custom mounting interface

A custom shaft or custom mounting interface can reduce additional adapters and simplify final product assembly.

## Step 9: Evaluate Noise, Heat, and Lifetime

Noise, heat, and lifetime are especially important in compact products.

Check:

* Gearbox noise under load
* Motor noise
* Vibration
* Heat generation
* Continuous working time
* Repeated start-stop cycles
* Load profile
* Operating temperature
* Lubrication condition
* Required lifetime
* Working environment

Prototype testing is recommended because compact mechanisms are sensitive to friction, alignment, load variation, and assembly tolerance.

## Common Selection Mistakes

Avoid these common mistakes during planetary gearbox selection:

| Mistake                          | Possible Result                                                        |
| -------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Selecting only by outer diameter | Torque, shaft, speed, or lifetime may not match the application        |
| Ignoring peak torque             | Gearbox may fail under acceleration, impact, or overload               |
| Choosing too high a gear ratio   | Output speed may be too slow and efficiency may decrease               |
| Ignoring backlash                | Positioning accuracy and repeatability may be poor                     |
| Ignoring duty cycle              | Heat and wear may reduce service life                                  |
| Ignoring shaft design            | Mechanical integration may require extra adapters                      |
| Ignoring noise requirement       | Product may be unsuitable for medical, lab, or indoor use              |
| Skipping prototype testing       | Real load friction and assembly tolerance may cause performance issues |
