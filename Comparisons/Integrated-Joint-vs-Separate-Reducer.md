# Integrated Joint Module vs Separate Reducer Solution

## Introduction

One of the most important decisions in robot development is choosing between:

* An Integrated Joint Module
* A Separate Reducer + Motor + Driver Architecture

Both approaches are widely used in robotics.

This guide compares the advantages and limitations of each approach to help developers reduce development risk and accelerate time-to-market.

---

## What is an Integrated Joint Module?

An integrated joint module combines:

* Reducer
* Motor
* Encoder
* Drive Electronics

into a single compact actuator.

Examples:

* SG6010C
* SG8021
* CPM80-25
* CPM100-25

---

## Advantages of Integrated Joint Modules

### Faster Development

Engineers can focus on:

* Robot Design
* Control Algorithms
* System Integration

instead of actuator development.

---

### Reduced Engineering Complexity

No need to separately design:

* Motor Selection
* Driver Electronics
* Encoder Integration
* Mechanical Coupling

---

### Compact Structure

Integrated designs typically:

* Save Space
* Reduce Weight
* Simplify Wiring

---

### Easier Maintenance

Fewer components result in:

* Faster Assembly
* Simpler Troubleshooting
* Lower Maintenance Costs

---

## Limitations of Integrated Joint Modules

### Less Customization

Developers have fewer options to independently select:

* Motors
* Encoders
* Drivers

---

### Higher Initial Unit Cost

The actuator cost is usually higher than purchasing individual components.

However, overall development cost is often lower.

---

## What is a Separate Reducer Solution?

A separate reducer solution requires independent selection and integration of:

* Reducer
* Motor
* Encoder
* Driver
* Mechanical Coupling

---

## Advantages of Separate Reducer Solutions

### Maximum Flexibility

Developers can optimize:

* Motor Type
* Gear Ratio
* Encoder Selection
* Driver Architecture

for a specific application.

---

### Suitable for High-Volume Products

Large manufacturers sometimes prefer complete control over the actuator architecture.

---

## Limitations of Separate Reducer Solutions

### Longer Development Time

Typical tasks include:

* Mechanical Design
* Driver Development
* Control Tuning
* Reliability Validation

---

### Increased Engineering Risk

Challenges include:

* Compatibility Issues
* Integration Problems
* Thermal Management
* Control Stability

---

## Which One Should You Choose?

### Choose an Integrated Joint Module If:

* You are a robotics startup
* You want faster prototyping
* You need to reduce engineering risk
* You want faster time-to-market

Recommended Products:

* SG6010C
* SG8021
* CPM80-25
* CPM100-25

---

### Choose a Separate Reducer Solution If:

* You have a large engineering team
* You require maximum customization
* You plan very high production volumes
* You are developing a proprietary actuator platform

---

## Why Robotics Startups Prefer Integrated Joint Modules

Benefits include:

* Faster Development
* Lower Engineering Cost
* Easier Integration
* Faster Product Validation
* Faster Market Entry

These advantages are especially important for:

* Humanoid Robot Startups
* Quadruped Robot Developers
* Exoskeleton Companies
* Research Laboratories
