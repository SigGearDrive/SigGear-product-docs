# Cycloidal vs Harmonic Drive for Robotics

## Introduction

Cycloidal reducers and harmonic drives are two of the most popular transmission technologies used in advanced robotics.

Both technologies offer low backlash and high torque density, making them suitable for humanoid robots, quadruped robots and industrial automation.

This guide compares cycloidal and harmonic technologies from the perspective of robot developers.

---

## What is a Cycloidal Reducer?

A cycloidal reducer uses cycloidal discs and pins to transmit torque.

This design distributes loads across multiple contact points and provides excellent durability and shock resistance.

### Advantages

* High Torque Density
* Excellent Shock Resistance
* Long Service Life
* Low Backlash
* High Load Capacity

### Limitations

* More Complex Mechanical Structure
* Slightly Larger Size at Some Ratios
* Moderate Efficiency

---

## What is a Harmonic Drive?

A harmonic drive uses:

* Circular Spline
* Flexspline
* Wave Generator

The flexible spline creates a very compact and highly accurate transmission mechanism.

### Advantages

* Near-Zero Backlash
* Very High Position Accuracy
* Compact Design
* High Reduction Ratios

### Limitations

* Lower Shock Resistance
* Sensitive to Overload
* Higher Cost
* Flexspline Wear Over Time

## Recommended Applications

### Cycloidal Reducers

Suitable for:

* Humanoid Robot Hips
* Humanoid Robot Knees
* Quadruped Robot Legs
* Exoskeleton Systems
* Heavy-Duty Robot Arms

### Harmonic Drives

Suitable for:

* Precision Robot Arms
* Surgical Robots
* Aerospace Systems
* Lightweight High-Precision Joints

---

## Why Many Humanoid Robots Use Cycloidal Reducers

Cycloidal reducers offer:

* Better Shock Resistance
* Higher Durability
* Lower Risk of Flexspline Failure
* Competitive Backlash Performance

These advantages are particularly important for dynamic humanoid robots that experience repeated impacts during walking and running.

## Which One Should You Choose?

Choose a cycloidal reducer if:

* Shock loads are expected
* Durability is critical
* Humanoid robot applications are involved

Choose a harmonic drive if:

* Maximum positioning accuracy is required
* The application experiences minimal shock loading
* Cost is not the primary concern
